Our Programmes

Structured sommelier education aligned with international standards and professional hospitality practice.

Certificate in Sommellerie & Service – Level 1

A comprehensive foundation programme combining viticulture, global wine knowledge, structured tasting methodology and professional service standards. Designed for hospitality professionals, aspiring sommeliers and serious wine students.

Programme Structure

  • 40 hours face-to-face instruction
  • 20+ hours guided independent study
  • In-person seminars, tutored tastings and service workshops
  • Aligned with ASI structured tasting methodology

Core Areas of Study

  • Viticulture & climate influences
  • Winemaking & maturation techniques
  • Principal international grape varieties
  • Classic and emerging wine regions
  • Sparkling, fortified & sweet wines
  • Professional wine service & guest interaction
  • Food & wine harmony
  • Beer, spirits, sake & global beverages

Assessment

  • Written theory examination
  • Blind tasting (ASI format)
  • Practical service assessment
  • Continuous coursework & portfolio

Pass mark: 60% overall.

Advanced Certificate in Professional Sommellerie – Level 2

An advanced professional programme designed to prepare candidates for ASI Certification 1 level. Focused on precision tasting, global wine mastery, fine-dining service execution and beverage list strategy.

Structure

  • 30+ hours mandatory online pre-course theory (Level 3 Diploma module)
  • 40 hours face-to-face advanced instruction
  • Service simulations & blind tasting intensives
  • Minimum 24 wines tasted in structured format

Advanced Focus Areas

  • Terroir-driven global appellations
  • Benchmark white & red wine evaluation
  • Advanced blind tasting methodology (ASI format)
  • Fine-dining service under exam conditions
  • Food pairing at professional level
  • Beverage list costing & cellar management
  • Guest psychology & leadership in hospitality

Assessment

  • Professional practical service exam
  • Blind tasting examination
  • Final written theory assessment
  • Food & wine pairing oral defense

Pass mark: 60% overall. All components must be attempted.
